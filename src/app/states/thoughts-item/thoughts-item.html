Why do outsourcing
white-labeled relationships fail?

I should start with the fact that I always hated «outsourcing» word - I can’t understand how being an outsourcing software development company might be a business goal. There are a lot of companies (particularly in Ukraine) who proudly position themselves as «outsourcing companies» and consider that part of their branding strategy (moreover there are even communities and successfully running conferences utilising this word). All right that may be a good start for a company just established, that is also a good way to keep your developers busy but that can’t be a permanent and ultimate goal (in my humble opinion at least).

The problem

To be completely clear I am talking here about the most common and most used cases of «outsourcing» in software world - proving development or other digital services under white-label agreement, essentially doing someone’s work instead of them. Let’s consider a few main cases why there may be a need for outsourcing some of your work under white-label agreement:

  1. You ARE ABLE to do the job in-house but you don’t have enough resources right now and you already have a commitment for the project. So you need to solve your problem by giving the work to a sub-contractor (without disclosing that to your client).
  2. You ARE ABLE to do the job in-house but you don’t have enough resources right now and you already have a commitment for the project. So you need to solve your problem by giving the work to a sub-contractor (without disclosing that to your client).

I totally respect the reasons and I can also accept the solution in the first case - it might happen that you are hiring a company similar to yours and it wouldn’t be wise enough to introduce your competitor to your client. Moreover that looks more like resolving a temporary issue than a way you build your business. However, the 2nd case is what often happens on a permanent basis and becomes a long-term relationship. I am totally confident that it’s a bad practice for both parties for the following reason:

The communication in outsourcing is always broken no matter how good it is

That happens just because there is someone in the middle of communication process passing the information back and forth and moreover that person is usually not skilled enough in the particular area. This leads to the situation when all parties spend too much time and therefore everyone lose money.
